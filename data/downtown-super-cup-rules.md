# Downtown Super Cup - Official Tournament Rules

## 1. GAME RULES

Tournament shall be governed by FIFA Laws of the Game as modified by US Club Soccer and as further modified herein.

## 2. SPORTSMANSHIP

Good sportsmanship is mandatory. Coaches and managers are responsible for the conduct of their team's players, parents, and other affiliated spectators. Coaches may only enter the field when signaled by referees. Haranguing, harassment, distraction, and offensive language are prohibited. Violations result in warnings followed by ejection, with the Tournament Director determining disciplinary measures.

## 3. CREDENTIALS

Valid US Club Soccer, USYS/CYSA, or AYSO passes required for all players and coaches. Maximum roster size is 26; maximum dressed players are 18 (11v11), 16 (9v9/7v7), and 12 (5v5). Required documents at each game include valid player passes, signed medical release forms, and guest/loan forms.

## 4. TEAM AGE

Team age follows the 2025/2026 season age under the Calendar Age Matrix.

## 5. TOURNAMENT CHECK-IN

Failure to check in will result in an automatic disqualification (without refund of fees). Online check-in must be completed before tournament start; failure results in a one-point deduction.

## 6. GAME CARDS

Only players on the game cards will be allowed to play. Rosters freeze Thursday evening before the event.

## 7. TEAMS ACCEPTANCE AND PLACEMENT IN DIVISIONS AND BRACKETS

Acceptance depends on prior results. Teams' placement in divisions and brackets is based on rankings and seeding as determined by the Tournament Director and the Tournament Committee. Withdrawal due to disagreement with placement forfeits refunds.

## 8. TOURNAMENT POINTS AWARDED IN PRELIMINARY PLAY

Point system for pool play:
- Win: 6 points
- Tie: 3 points
- Loss: 0 points
- 1 point per goal (maximum 3 per game)
- 1 point for shutout (including 0-0 ties)
- Forfeit winner: 10 points (scored 3-0)
- Both teams forfeit: 0 points each
- Red card deduction: 1 point (player), 2 points (coach/manager)

## 9. TIE BREAKING PROCEDURES -- TOURNAMENT POINTS

Tiebreakers applied in order:
1. Head-to-head results
2. Most total wins
3. Goal differential (maximum 4 per game)
4. Most goals scored (maximum 5 per game)
5. Fewest goals allowed
6. Most shutouts
7. Fewest red card ejections
8. Penalty kicks (if both teams present)
9. Coin toss by Tournament Director

For 3-way ties, GD/GF limits (4/5) are not applied.

## 10. POST BRACKET PLAY PAIRING

Teams matched in bracket play avoid preliminary opponents when possible. Only the lowest-point qualifiers will be swapped. Under no circumstances the top seeds will be paired in these games.

## 11. LOGISTICS

Teams occupy designated sidelines; spectators remain opposite with restrictions on goal-line placement. Each team must have at least one properly credentialed adult present on the sidelines of each match.

## 12. GAME LENGTH

- U6-U10: 2 x 20 minutes
- U11-U12 (9v9): 2 x 25 minutes
- U13-19 (11v11): 2 x 30 minutes (Fall 2025 U13-U19 plays 7v7: 2x20 minutes)

## 13. HOME TEAM

In preliminary rounds, the first-listed team is home. In finals, the higher-seeded team is home. Coin toss breaks ties. Home team wears alternate jerseys if colors clash.

## 14. GAME START

All games will start at the scheduled time. Teams must report to the Field Marshal 30 minutes before each game for check-in. Teams must field minimum players (7/6/5/3 for 11v11/9v9/7v7/5v5) within five minutes or forfeit. Preliminary games end five minutes before the next scheduled start. A game is complete after at least one full half. Ties allowed in preliminary play.

## 15. HALF TIME

Halftime lasts exactly 5 minutes; the Tournament Director may extend under special circumstances. Field Marshals may adjust game length. Regulation ties recorded as ties (except Championship & Consolation games).

## 16. TIE BREAKING PROCEDURES FOR GAMES

Quarterfinals, semifinals, and 3rd-place games use penalty kicks to determine winners. Small-sided games (U8-U12) use 3 penalty kicks instead of 5. Championship games that end in a tie at the end of regulation time will be extended by two 5-minute overtime periods with no rest period between them. Golden Goal applies. Penalty kicks follow if still tied after overtime.

## 17. SUSPENDED AND TERMINATED GAMES

Tournament Officials may suspend/terminate games for cause. Terminated games may resume at officials' discretion but must end five minutes before the next scheduled start. If in the opinion of Tournament officials, a game must be terminated due to the serious or violent misconduct of players, coaches, or spectators; the offending team may be immediately suspended from further play.

Game delays only permitted for serious injuries. If more than a quarter of preliminary game cannot be completed due to a serious injury suspending game play, the game shall be determined by penalty kicks.

## 18. SUBSTITUTIONS

Unlimited substitutions during a game are allowed at the discretion of the referee prior to any restart.

## 19. SPECIAL RULES FOR SMALL-SIDED DIVISIONS

Follow US Soccer Player Development Initiatives rules. NO HEADING RULE WILL APPLY to all small sided games. For 7v7, incorrect throw-ins may be retaken. Penalty tiebreakers limited to 3 kicks.

## 20. EJECTIONS AND CAUTIONS

Ejected individuals cannot return that game and miss the next scheduled game. Ejections will be reviewed by the Tournament Committee and may result in a more severe penalty, including additional game suspensions in the tournament. Fighting, violent conduct, and referee harassment are serious misconduct. Ejections result in point deductions: 1 point (player), 2 points (coach/spectator).

## 21. DISPUTES

Game conduct is under the total jurisdiction of the referee. The tournament will not overrule a referee's decision.

## 22. PROTESTS

All game results will be considered final. No protests will be allowed.

## 23. FORFEIT AND BYES

All teams that forfeit will have the game(s) scored a 0-3 loss. The winner will be awarded ten (10) tournament points. Teams failing to report within five minutes forfeit.

## 24. GAME BALLS

Home team provides balls (subject to referee approval). Size 4 for U8-U12; Size 5 for U13+.

## 25. PLAYER EQUIPMENT

**Cleats:** Only soccer cleats or turf shoes allowed; no metal studs on turf.

**Shinguards:** Required; professionally manufactured, unaltered, covering minimum 3/4 of shin but not exceeding 10 inches.

**Casts/Splints:** Players wearing any dangerous equipment or device including orthopedic casts, air splints, or metal splints, shall not be eligible to participate.

**Braces:** Discouraged but allowed at referee's discretion if safe. Elastic supports without metal/carbon fiber permitted if non-hazardous.

**Eyeglasses:** Sports goggles encouraged; if worn, must have unbreakable lenses/frames.

**Jewelry:** Except for Medical Alert Warning Bracelets (which are to be taped as necessary), absolutely no jewelry may be worn.

## 26. ARTIFICIAL TURF FIELDS

No food will be allowed onto the fields, particularly seeds of any kind or gum. Violators risk ejection from the facility.

## 27. ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES

Alcoholic beverages at all tournament venues are prohibited, without exception.

## 28. NO LITTERING

Teams must collect and dispose of all garbage in provided receptacles.

## 29. MEDICAL ASSISTANCE

All injuries shall be reported to the Tournament Director or Field Marshal so that an Accident Report Form can be completed. Emergency Services called for serious injuries upon request.

## 30. WEATHER

Winners determined by points earned in completed/begun games if inclement weather occurs. In case of cancellation due to weather, tournament officials retain the right to keep up to one-third (1/3) of the registration fee to cover expenses. There will be no make-up games due to weather.

## 31. RULES CHANGES

The Tournament Committee reserves the right to modify these rules at any time prior to the start of the Tournament. Changes announced at team check-in. Committee resolves unlisted situations; decisions are final.

## 32. WITHDRAWAL

Written withdrawal requests addressed to the Tournament Director incur penalties ranging from $100 to full fee loss. Late applications assessed $75 penalty.

## 33. OTHER

The Tournament Committee will make all other determinations.
